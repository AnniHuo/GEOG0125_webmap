"SMART" FAST FOOD DISTRIBUTION




Food type availability could largely influence the obesity disparities across metropolitan regions. It has become a common phenomenon among office workers to consume fast food, which is associated with malnutrition and obesity, further causing low performance and absenteeism. Therefore, the distribution of fast-food outlets in London might seriously affect the obesity epidemic among workers. The interactive maps bellow will examine the current distribution of fast-food outlets and integrate with employment condition in administrative areas in London to discover potential relations, in order to benefit future workplace health intervention.




Map Items


The choropleth map layers have represented the employment rates in London at different levels (including Middle Layer Super Output Area (MSOA)-level and Lower Layer Super Output Areas (LSOA)-level). Besides, the fast-food outlets locations have been displayed by ‘black’ points. By switching the layers, the relationships between fast-food outlets and employment condition at different administrative levels can be apparently discovered by changing overlay choices. The map uses quintiles to divide gradient colors (0.44, 0.48, 0.51, 0.55), which are the same at these administrative levels.



Insights


It is apparent that areas with higher fast-food density also have higher employment rate. Overall, this phenomenon reflects the location selection strategy of the fast-food restaurants to a certain extent and indicates the unhealthy environment exposure for workers. In that case, local planning regulations should be taken as a weapon to benefit them.
